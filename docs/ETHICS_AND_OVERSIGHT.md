# Ethics and Oversight

## Purpose

Define how Canoniga separates research assistance from clinical decision support and diagnosis.

## Role definitions

| Role | Canoniga behavior |
|------|-------------------|
| Research assistance | Summarize evidence, contradictions, and testable next steps for scientists |
| Scientific discovery support | Rank hypotheses and repurposing opportunities with explicit uncertainty |
| Clinical decision support | **Not provided** — outputs must not be used as patient-specific treatment guidance |
| Medical diagnosis | **Not provided** — no disease classification for individuals |

## Ethical boundaries

- Prioritize evidence over authority
- Preserve contradictory findings instead of collapsing them
- Never present conjecture as established fact
- Require human review for high-impact promotion paths

## Data handling defaults

- Local-first storage with investigator-controlled databases
- Authentication and audit trails for user activity
- No mandatory cloud data exfiltration for core workflows

## Medical limitations

Outputs may be incomplete, biased by publication patterns, or affected by extraction error. All conclusions require experimental validation and qualified human oversight.

## Oversight expectation

Deploying organizations should assign a scientific owner responsible for:

- Review queue triage
- Signoff policy enforcement
- Periodic benchmark and regression gate review
